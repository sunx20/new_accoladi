Letters of Introduction

The following are letters that students have written to collegiate ensemble directors and studio instructors denoting their desire to be one of their future students:

Ms. Horne,

I sing! I sing Gospel, I sing Broadway, I sing Opera, I sing Classical, I sing Pop, I sing R&B, I sing Choral, and I sing Blues. I am confident if you would become my voice instructor I would do it all better!

Just Between Us Singers,

Ebony Stackhouse

Dr. William,

I’m Sasha Connor, a rising junior at Greene Central High School, in Snow Hill, North Carolina. Snow Hill is a small town with less than 1,000 people in Eastern, North Carolina and in all my sixteen years of living here I’ve only seen it snow here once. My life centers around school, church, and piano. In fact, I am the accompanist for my high school chorus, and I play for every function at the First Baptist Church.

I’ve been taking piano lessons since I was three. I started with the pianist at the First Baptist Church, and I took lessons from her until I was nine. She passed away and I then became the church pianist. The church paid for me to start taking piano lessons at East Carolina University in nearby Greenville. I started taking from the head of the piano faculty and did so until last year. He helped improve my technique and started having me compete in piano contests. In fact, I’ve placed first in every competition I’ve entered. He suggested that I change teachers last year to the new piano instructor who is a touring concert pianist. When she is not teaching at the university she is traveling, performing as a recitalist and symphony soloist. With her help last year, I won the East Carolina Piano Festival Competition by performing Bela Bartok’s ‘Allegro Barbaro .’ The prize for winning this competition is performing a concerto with the Long Bay Symphony in Myrtle Beach, South Carolina. I am working on the Alec Rawley’s ‘Miniature Concerto’ for this concert.

It is my passion to someday perform the Edvard Grieg’s ‘Concerto in A Minor.’ I see you have performed it many times with numerous symphonies across the world. If you are ever giving a Master Class on this concerto please let me know and I’ll be there. In addition, I have watched all your performances you’ve posted on YouTube.com and would be deeply honored to be considered to be a student in your studio. Can you share with me the best path to one of these coveted spots?

Hoping to Be One of Your Most Celebrated Students One Day,

Sasha O’Connor

Dr. Wu,

I’m a rising senior percussionist at a fine arts high school in Florida. I’ve been selected the past two years as a member of the Florida All-State Band, and I have been selected to be one of the four percussionists with the American Youth Philharmonic Orchestra that will be performing at Carnegie Hall and touring China this summer. All these experiences, along with my personal commitment to becoming an excellent performer, have inspired my interest in becoming a professional performing percussionist.

My research reveals that this was your life’s path for many years, and then you became an instructor to mentor young percussionists like myself. I am hoping this fall to secure an invitation to one of your weekend sessions on “Professional Percussionists Have Mastered It All.” Also, know how very much I want to be a part of your studio next year. If you have suggestions on how I can prepare for the audition, please send me an email. Thank you for giving my request your personal attention.

Sincerely,

Donald Lloyd

Dr. McIntyre,

I love band! It’s my whole life. I live for band every day. I’m not the best trumpet player in Texas, but I am the best in my high school band. I have not been able to take private lessons other than the ones from my band director, Mr. Richardson. He gives so much to his students, and many times he has shared that everything he learned about teaching he learned from you. I also want to learn from you so that I can be a great and caring band director like Mr. Richardson. What are the best steps I can take to be in your band and become an instrumental music education major on your campus next year?

Thanks,

Russell Blaylock

Dr. Littleton,

I’m a homeschooled student and have played cello since I was in the 4th grade. My mother plays violin, my dad plays double bass, and they’ve taught me almost every day. I’m just a junior but I’m interested in becoming one of your students now and getting a head start on what it will be like when I’m one of your students my freshman year at TSU. When, and how, can we begin establishing this student – teacher relationship?

Looking Forward to Hearing Back,

Mia Floyd
