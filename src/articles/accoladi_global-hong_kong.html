Accoladi Global: Hong Kong

Overview

If you are looking to attend college in a large and diverse city, Hong Kong could be a viable option for you. English is seen as a co-first language in the eyes of Hong Kong's education system, so all of the lectures at most universities are given in English. Campus life in Hong Kong is comparable to that of American universities, and non-local students have several resources to help make them feel more at home. Attending a university in Hong Kong could be a transformative experience in one's life, but how much would it cost to spend four years overseas?

Luckily, Hong Kong universities are comparable in cost to American public universities and in many cases, are even cheaper.  To combat the price of tuition and living expenses for international students, the Government of Hong Kong has set up the Government Scholarship Fund. This fund offers foreign students $10,300 USD per year, covering many expenses and making Hong Kong universities more affordable for international students. More information on this scholarship can be found here: HKSAR Government Scholarship Fund.

Visa Requirements

To study in Hong Kong, you will need to obtain a student visa. Unlike most countries, you are not required to have health insurance in order to obtain a Hong Kong student visa, but some institutions may require you to be covered.  Additionally, the “sponsor” requirement can be fulfilled by your institution rather than a local individual.  However, you must show that you are able to financially support yourself while you study in Hong Kong without having to work a job.  This can include bank statements, cash on hand, traveler’s cheques, credit cards, scholarships, etc. You also must pay a $30 visa application fee.  The processing time for a Hong Kong student visa is 6 weeks, but some institutions recommend that you complete the process through the institution itself so that the processing time can be shortened.  You can visit Students | Immigration Department (immd.gov.hk) for more information on the process of attaining a study visa to Hong Kong.

Hong Kong Compared to US Colleges

Hong Kong Baptist University vs Belmont University

Both Hong Kong Baptist University (HKBU) and Belmont University have comprehensive music programs and are around the same size.  At HKBU, students can take one of three pathways toward their bachelor’s degree.  The first is the BA in Music; under this program, students earn a BA in four years and choose one of the following for a concentration: composition/music production, performance/pedagogy, directed studies, or music education.  The second is the BM in Creative Industries program, where students earn their degree in four years and choose either Scoring for Film, Television, and Video Games, or Popular Music Performance and Songwriting as a concentration.  The third path is pursuing an Associate’s Degree in Music Studies in 2 years and afterward pursuing a BA in Music Studies in an additional 2 years.  The AD/BA degree path is a more generalized curriculum than the specialized BA in Music and BM in Creative Industries.  HKBU also offers a plethora of performing opportunities through its many vocal and instrumental ensembles.

Similar to HKBU, Belmont offers a choice between a BA in Music and a BM.  For Belmont’s BA in Music, students receive broad training in Classical music and combine this with another field by choosing a minor in any of Belmont’s other BA fields.  Additionally, students in this BA program will study an instrument of their choice.  The second option is Belmont’s BM degree program.  At Belmont, students can earn a BM in Church Music, Commercial Music, Composition, Music Education, Music Performance, Music Technology, Music Theory, Music Therapy, Music Theory, Musical Theatre, or Piano Pedagogy.  Belmont also offers oodles of performance opportunities in its many ensembles.

Overall, HKBU and Belmont offer similar programs and opportunities with Belmont offering more specialized degree programs.  However, they differ greatly in terms of cost.  Belmont has an annual tuition of $38,430, with an estimated total annual cost (including room, board, books, travel, meals, and personal expenses) of $54,670.  HKBU, on the other hand, charges $18,032 annually for tuition, bringing the total annual cost to $25,462, before scholarships and financial aid.

University of Hong Kong vs Chapman University

Chapman University, located in Orange, CA, and the University of Hong Kong (HKU)  are comparable both in terms of size and musical programs.  At 7,500 and 9,000 undergraduates, respectively, these two institutions both have small but well-funded music schools and prioritize performance opportunities for music majors.

Chapman University offers four degrees in music: BA in Music, Bachelor’s of Music (Pre-Music Education Certification), BM in Composition, and BM in Performance.  Chapman’s BA in Music, like most institutions, offers a broad curriculum on music theory and history, but Chapman is intentional in its inclusion of ethnomusicology.  The other three degrees offered are specifically designed for particular fields: music education, composition, and performance.  Notably, the BM in Performance allows for students to have a concentration in conducting, which is a rarity in American bachelor’s degree programs.

HKU only offers a BA in Music with three options for concentrations.  Despite this, HKU’s department of music is seen as the best music school in all of Hong Kong. Their degree program covers all aspects of music to ensure that each student receives a well-rounded education and that their view on music is expanded as a whole. In addition to these general courses, the music department offers numerous courses that are specific to performance, and there is even a course specifically offered to international students for music performance.  Additionally, HKU ranks in the top 100 universities in the world, meaning that students attending HKU receive some of the best education in the world.

Undoubtedly, Chapman and HKU are both quality universities that offer their students many opportunities to learn and demonstrate their learning.  However, students will save money by attending HKU over a school like Chapman while receiving an as good or better education.  Chapman University charges $58,250 in annual tuition with an estimated annual cost of $71,330.  On the other hand, The University of Hong Kong charges $22,025 in annual tuition, bringing the annual cost to $32,327, and this doesn’t even account for the many music scholarships that HKU awards, for which international students are eligible.

Hong Kong Academy for the Performing Arts vs San Francisco Conservatory of Music

Hong Kong Academy for the Performing Arts (HKAPA) and the San Francisco Conservatory of Music are both very small schools that focus solely on the performing arts, and both offer numerous degrees in those areas.  

HKAPA offers a unique slate of degree programs; they offer BM degrees, as well as BA’s in Chinese Opera, Dance, Drama, Film & TV, and Theatre & Entertainment Arts.  Further, BM students may major in performance of an instrument of their choice, Chinese Music, Composition & Electronic Music, Keyboard, Strings, Vocal Studies, Woodwind, Brass, & Percussion, and Academic Studies.  San Francisco’s curriculum is very similar, allowing students to major in performance, jazz and American music, and notably, conducting, among others.  Overall, both offer phenomenal training in composition, performance, and musicology that will empower students to be successful musicians.

Despite their similarity in curriculum and quality, HKAPA is far more affordable for the everyday American than San Francisco Conservatory of Music.  While San Francisco charges $49,300 in annual tuition, HKAPA only charges $6,826, bringing the estimated total annual cost to be $76,675 and $17,208, respectively.

Conclusion

As the tables show, a music student can get a similar education to that of the United States in Hong Kong for less money.  In addition, studying in Hong Kong will expose you to Chinese and Southeast Asian cultures and give you experiences you won’t find in the United States.  Furthermore, you can even study Chinese music at many of these institutions and gain a more in-depth perspective as a musician.
