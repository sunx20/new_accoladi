Help

Program Overview:

Accoladi.com lets you showcase your abilities and experiences in such a way that will attract college recruiters. When completed, your student profile will present your talents to individuals looking for students just like you. Filling out this profile completely will help you to communicate all of your musical accomplishments to colleges across the country.

Festivals and Competitions:
The Festivals and Competitions section of your portfolio lets you showcase events you have participated in throughout your musical career. Options to add performance dates, locations and video footage allow college recruiters to more thoroughly understand your musical upbringing by watching your growth over time.

General Performances:
The General Performances section of your portfolio gives you the freedom to display all of your miscellaneous repertoire. This section can be used to showcase your best playing from audition material to concert performances regardless of setting.

Honors and Awards:
The Honors and Awards section of your portfolio gives you an avenue to present all of your musical recognition. This includes awards presented by your high school program, admission into audition-based ensembles, scholarships or any other form of distinction that shows college recruiters a pattern of success in your musical endeavors.

Masterclasses:
The Master Classes section of your portfolio lets you present your history of directed learning from reputable instructors. Whether or not you were critiqued gives recruiters a great deal of insight as well. If your study was under industry leaders or known colleagues, name recognition of professors or institutions in this section can mean a great deal to recruiters who value the importance of expert advice.

Private Studies:
The Private Studies section of your portfolio is a fantastic way to show recruiters how specialized you are on your instrument. In addition to adding how long you studied, you can also enter with whom you studied. The Institution tab lets you articulate where you teacher works to establish their credibility if your professor does not know them by name. This can mean a lot to applied faculty because they are well aware that great teachers produce great students. Summer Enrichment:

Summer Enrichment:
The Summer Enrichment section of your portfolio lets you showcase your summer efforts in a way that is appealing to college recruiters. You can tell your story by entering the names, institutions and lengths associated with your musical expeditions. This section gives you the chance to personalize your story by showcasing your growth outside of the classroom whether it be playing or teaching at a camp, touring with a corp or performing with any other entity.
