Musical Theater

Standard Audition Requirements

All applicants to collegiate musical theater programs are required to record a standard audition video which includes the content outlined below.

  • Each performance segment must be recorded in one take with no editing (though you may and should select the best of several takes).
  • Professional equipment and/or studio space is not necessary. With attention to a few details, your music making can be conveyed using readily available consumer electronics; even iPhone or other smartphone recordings are acceptable.
  • Please refer to the article “7 Steps in Making a Great Audition Video” for additional information and suggestions for effectively recording your audition.

Required Video Segments for Musical Theater

Introduction: Before you record your first musical selection, please include a spoken introduction in which you state your name, instrument, hometown, and state. Then announce the title of the song and composer and/or arranger before you begin the performance for each required tune/style/excerpt.

Musical Selections: You are asked to record only one video.

Two musical selections and one monologue are required. It is suggested that the two selections come from the following stylistic Musical Theater expressions:

  • One Music Theater ballad
  • One Music Theater patter song
  • One Monologue

    • The Monologue, not to exceed two-minutes, is to come from published plays or musicals. (Monologues from television, movies, or other mediums are not allowed)

Note that adding a dance number is optional on the audition video.

Premier Audition Requirements

All applicants to collegiate music programs are required to record an audition video which includes the content outlined below.

  • Each performance segment must be recorded in one take with no editing (though you may and should select the best of several takes).
  • Professional equipment and/or studio space is not necessary. With attention to a few details, your music making can be conveyed using readily available consumer electronics; even iPhone or other smartphone recordings are acceptable.
  • Please refer to the article “7 Steps in Making a Great Audition Video” for additional information and suggestions for recording your audition.

Required Video Segments for Musical Theater

Introduction: Before you record your first musical selection, please include a spoken introduction in which you state your name, instrument, hometown, and state. Then announce the title of the song and composer and/or arranger before you begin the performance for each required tune/style/excerpt.

Required Music and Monologue Selections: You are asked to record only one video. Three musical selections plus two monologues are required. It is suggested that the two selections come from the following stylistic Musical Theater expressions:

  • One classic Music Theater piece written prior to 1970
  • One Music Theater patter song
  • One Music Theater ballad written after the 1970s
  • One Music Theater selection of your choice
  • Two contrasting monologues
  • The two selections together should not exceed three minutes in total time.
  • The monologues are to come from published plays or musicals. (Monologues from television, movies, or other mediums are not allowed)
  • In addition, you are asked to name the show this number is from and the character performing this number in the show.
  • Applicants may perform unaccompanied, with a live rhythm section, or with prerecorded rhythm tracks.
  • In reference to the monologue, you are asked to share the title of the monologue.

Note that adding a dance number is optional on the audition video.
