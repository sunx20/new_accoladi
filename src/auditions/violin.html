Violin

Standard Audition Requirements

All applicants to collegiate music programs are required to record a standard audition video which includes the content outlined below.

  • Each performance segment must be recorded in one take with no editing (though you may and should select the best of several takes).
  • Professional equipment and/or studio space is not necessary. With attention to a few details, your music making can be conveyed using readily available consumer electronics; even iPhone or other smartphone recordings are acceptable.
  • Please refer to the article “7 Steps in Making a Great Audition Video” for additional information and suggestions for effectively recording your audition.

Required Video Segments for Violin

Introduction: Before you record your first musical selection, please include a spoken introduction in which you state your name, instrument, hometown, and state. Then announce the title of the song and composer and/or arranger before you begin the performance for each required tune/style/excerpt.

Musical Selections: You are asked to record only one video. Auditionees must perform at least two contrasting selections. It is suggested that the two selections come from the following stylistic expressions:

  • A movement of a Concerto (with applicable cadenza)
  • Two contrasting movements from an unaccompanied Bach sonata or partita
  • A complete Classical Sonata
  • A complete Romantic work from the 19th century
  • A complete work from the 20th or 21st century
  • One piece of the auditionee’s choice

Optional: Memorization is not required but highly suggested on at least one of the selections.

If you wish to demonstrate proficiency on a doubling or secondary instrument, you must include one or two additional examples.

Required Video Segments for Violin

Introduction: Before you record your first musical selection, please include a spoken introduction in which you state your name, instrument, hometown, and state. Then announce the title of the song and composer and/or arranger before you begin the performance for each required tune/style/excerpt.

Required Musical Selections: You are asked to record only one video. From the list detailed below, auditionees must perform two contrasting selections PLUS any two of the orchestral excerpts:

  • A movement of a Concerto (with applicable cadenza)
  • Two contrasting movements from an unaccompanied Bach sonata or partita
  • A complete Classical Sonata
  • A complete Romantic work from the 19th century
  • A complete work from the 20th or 21st century
  • A caprice (suggested Paganini) or an etude from one of the standard etude books, such as those of Paganini, Kreutzer, Fiorillo, Rode, or Dont
  • Any two of the following orchestral excerpts:
  • Beethoven, Symphony #9, (Violin I) 3rd Movement, mm.99-104
  • Rachmaninoff, Symphony #2, (Violin II) 2nd Movement, 15 bars after [32] – 4 bars before [35]
  • Prokofiev, “Classical” Symphony, 1st Movement, beginning to [H]
  • Stravinsky, The Rite of Spring, “Danse de la terre,” bars 72-79
  • Rossini, William Tell Overture, mm 316-359
  • Adams, Atomic Symphony, 1st Movement, mm. 66-113
  • Bartok, Concerto for Orchestra, 1st Movement, mm.76-135 and 476-end
  • One piece of the auditionee’s choice

Optional: Memorization is not required but highly suggested on at least one of the selections.

If you wish to demonstrate proficiency on a doubling or secondary instrument, you must include one or two additional examples.
