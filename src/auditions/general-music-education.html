General Music Education

Standard Audition Requirements

All applicants to collegiate music programs are required to record a standard audition video which includes the content outlined below.

  • Each performance segment must be recorded in one take with no editing (though you may and should select the best of several takes).
  • Professional equipment and/or studio space is not necessary. With attention to a few details, your music making can be conveyed using readily available consumer electronics; even iPhone or other smartphone recordings are acceptable.
  • Please refer to the article “7 Steps in Making a Great Audition Video” for additional information and suggestions for effectively recording your audition.

Required Video Segments for General Music Education

Introduction: Before you record your first musical selection, please include a spoken introduction in which you state your name, instrument/voice part, hometown, and state. Then announce the title of the song and composer and/or arranger before you begin the performance for each required tune/style/excerpt.

Musical Selections: You are asked to record only one video.

Perform at least two selections from below, denoting the required selection:

  • One performance piece of choice based on primary music discipline: vocal or instrumental. (If instrumental denote if keyboard, wind instrument, string instrument, or percussion instrument)
  • Perform an original song written specifically for children. (optional)
  • (Required) Perform two of the following children’s songs with Kodaly/Solfege hand signs and solfege syllables first, and then sing unaccompanied and/or you may accompany yourself on keyboard, guitar, or instrument of choice: “Twinkle, Twinkle Little Star,” “Are You Sleeping,” “This Old Man,” “Baa-Baa Black Sheep,” “Hush Little Baby,” “Tale As Old As Time,” “A Whole New World,” “Happy,” and “Let There Be Peace On Earth.”

If you wish to demonstrate proficiency on a doubling or secondary instrument, you must include one or two additional examples.

Required Video Segments for General Music Education

Introduction: Before you record your first musical selection, please include a spoken introduction in which you state your name, instrument/voice part, hometown, and state. Then announce the title of the song and composer and/or arranger before you begin the performance for each required tune/style/excerpt.

Required Musical Selections: You are asked to record only one video. Perform at least one selection for each of the first three denoted below, along with the required Kodaly/Solfege examples:

  • One performance piece of choice based on primary music discipline: vocal or instrumental. (If instrumental denote if keyboard, wind instrument, string instrument, or percussion instrument)
  • Perform two of the following children’s songs with Kodaly/Solfege hand signs and solfege syllables first, and then sing unaccompanied and/or you may accompany yourself on keyboard, guitar, or instrument of choice: “Twinkle, Twinkle Little Star,” “Are You Sleeping,” “This Old Man,” “Baa-Baa Black Sheep,” “Hush Little Baby,” “Tale As Old As Time,” “A Whole New World,” “Happy,” or “Let There Be Peace On Earth.”
  • Perform an original song written specifically for children. (optional)
  • Required - using Kodaly/Solfege hand signs and syllables sing:
    • a major scale both ascending and descending
    • a natural minor scale both ascending and descending
    • a harmonic minor scale both ascending and descending
    • the simple melody of “Morning Has Broken”

If you wish to wish to demonstrate proficiency on a doubling or secondary instrument, you must include one or two additional examples.
