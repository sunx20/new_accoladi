Tenor Trombone

Standard Audition Requirements

All applicants to collegiate music programs are required to record a standard audition video which includes the content outlined below.

  • Each performance segment must be recorded in one take with no editing (though you may and should select the best of several takes).
  • Professional equipment and/or studio space is not necessary. With attention to a few details, your music making can be conveyed using readily available consumer electronics; even iPhone or other smartphone recordings are acceptable.
  • Please refer to the article “7 Steps in Making a Great Audition Video” for additional information and suggestions for effectively recording your audition.

Required Video Segments for Tenor Trombone

Introduction: Before you record your first musical selection, please include a spoken introduction in which you state your name, instrument, hometown, and state. Then announce the title of the song and composer and/or arranger before you begin the performance for each required tune/style/excerpt.

Musical Selections: You are asked to record only one video. Auditionees must be prepared to perform two contrasting selections. It is suggested that the two selections come from the following stylistic expressions:

  • A movement of a Concerto (Suggested: Grondahl, Jacob, Wagenseil, Rimskij-Korsakov, David, Roto, and Wagner)
  • A complete work from the Classical or Romantic period
  • A complete work from the 20th or 21st century
  • An etude (Suggested: Bordohni/Rochut, Kopprasch, Blazhevich)
  • Any piece of the auditionee’s choice

Optional: Memorization is not required but highly suggested on at least one of the selections.

If you wish to demonstrate proficiency on a doubling or secondary instrument, you must include one or two additional examples.

Required Video Segments for Tenor Trombone

Introduction: Before you record your first musical selection, please include a spoken introduction in which you state your name, instrument, hometown, and state. Then announce the title of the song and composer and/or arranger before you begin the performance for each required tune/style/excerpt.

Required Musical Selections: You are asked to record only one video. From the list detailed below, auditionees must perform two contrasting selections PLUS any two of the orchestral or wind ensemble excerpts:

  • A movement of a Concerto (Suggested: Grondahl, Jacob, Wagenseil, Rimskij-Korsakov, David, Roto, and Wagner)
  • A Sonata (Suggested: Hendemith, Sulek, Casterede, Galliard, and Hetzler)
  • A complete work from the 20th or 21st century
  • An etude (Suggested: Bordohni/Rochut, Kopprasch, and Blazhevich)
  • Any two of the following orchestral or wind ensemble excerpts:
    • Stravinsky, Suite from Pulcinella, “VII.Duetto,” mm.85-93
    • Schoenberg, Symphony #2, Migrations, mm.405-428
    • Mahler, Symphony #3, Movement #1, mm. 13-17
    • Bernstein, Symphonic Dances from West Side Story, mm.524-541
  • Ravel, Bolero, solo, two bars after Section 10-11
  • Berlioz, Hungarian March, one measure before Section 20-two measures at Section 21
  • Arnold, Tam O’Shanter Overture, 15 measures before Section N
  • Any piece of the auditionee’s choice

Optional: Memorization is not required but highly suggested on at least one of the selections.

If you wish to wish to demonstrate proficiency on a doubling or secondary instrument, you must include one or two additional examples.
